Greater Los Angeles Wildfires

What's Happening in California? The Los Angeles wildfires are a recurring natural disaster exacerbated by the region's hot, dry climate and strong Santa Ana winds. These wildfires, often sparked by human activity or natural causes like lightning, spread rapidly through the city's chaparral-covered hills and suburban neighborhoods. The fires not only destroy homes and infrastructure but also threaten wildlife habitats and contribute to air quality issues due to heavy smoke and particulate matter. Climate change has intensified their frequency and severity, with longer fire seasons and drier vegetation acting as fuel. Efforts to mitigate wildfire risks include controlled burns, strict building codes, and public education campaigns about fire safety and evacuation procedures.

Interesting Facts: As of Thursday, 783 incarcerated firefighters were working around the clock to help slow the spread of the massive blazes as part of the California Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation Fire Camp Program.The state is one of at least a dozen that operate these fire camps and deploy incarcerated people to fight fires.The program pays prisoners up to $10.24 per day with the opportunity to earn more during active emergencies.In order to be eligible for the fire program, prisoners have to be deemed physically and mentally fit, they must have eight years or less on their sentence, and they cannot have convictions like arson or sexual violence.

How to Prevent Wildfires:

  • Practice Safe Fire Use
  • Dispose of Flammable Materials Properly
  • Maintain Vegetation and Landscaping
  • Follow Regulations for Burning
  • Report Suspicious Activity or Hazards
